## Research Contributions

### Existing Workflows and Integration Points

Writing should sound like a competent internal colleague who understands where the recommendation lands.

The note should not sound like a central team issuing instructions or a marketer pushing a campaign.

### Consulting Operating Model

Writing should sound aware of consulting roles without overexplaining them:

- relationship owner
- engagement lead
- practice expert
- delivery owner
- proposal lead

Use the role only when it clarifies control or next step.

### Consultant Problem-Solving and Meeting Prep

Writing should be answer-first and meeting-prep useful:

- state the signal
- state the likely client implication
- state what the other consultant can sharpen
- state the next decision or question

### Consultant Delivery Formats and Diagnosis

Writing should connect the recommendation to the output when relevant:

- "before the readout"
- "before the proposal goes out"
- "while the roadmap is still being sequenced"
- "before scoring the risk"
- "before turning this into a client-facing recommendation"

### Business Models, Acquisition, and Recurring Sales

Use client-helping language:

- "worth a quick check"
- "may help before the review"
- "could clarify the client risk"
- "if useful, you can decide whether to involve them"

Avoid revenue-first language:

- "upsell"
- "cross-sell"
- "wallet share"
- "land and expand"
- "high-propensity"
- "underpenetrated"

### Partner-Vendor Ecosystem

Writing should avoid vendor-pitch language. It should not sound like a reseller email.

Name vendor context only when it helps the account owner understand the client reason, and keep sensitive commercial mechanics out of the note by default.

### AI, Automation, Industry, And Signal Quality

Writing should avoid hype:

- no generic AI/productivity claims
- no compliance guarantees
- no fake industry specificity
- no "the system found" phrasing unless system provenance matters

### Consulting Collaboration Incentives and Blockers

Writing should make collaboration feel safe:

- the relationship owner is helped, not overruled
- the collaborator is positioned as a resource, not a salesperson
- the ask sounds small enough for a busy consultant to accept
- the note makes room for "no" if the angle is weak
- the language avoids credit, territory, ownership, and compensation politics

## Style Guidance

Use:

- short paragraphs
- concrete nouns
- explicit caveats
- "may be worth"
- "quick internal check"
- "before deciding whether to involve them"
- "before the readout"
- "before the proposal goes out"
- "to pressure-test the hypothesis"
- "pressure-test this before the meeting"
- "if the angle is useful"
- "no client intro unless you see a fit"

Avoid:

- "must"
- "should definitely"
- "unlock"
- "monetize"
- "AI-powered transformation"
- "game changer"
- "bring them into your client"
- "this is a cross-sell opportunity"

## Draft Writing Rule Copy

`Write like a thoughtful colleague helping the relationship owner decide whether another consultant's expertise is useful. Lead with the client situation, then the consultant fit, then how it helps the relationship owner, then the smallest safe ask.`

`If the useful help is a practice, team, solution asset, partner owner, or workflow owner rather than one person, name that plainly.`

## Tone

Plain, compact, specific, and internally respectful.
