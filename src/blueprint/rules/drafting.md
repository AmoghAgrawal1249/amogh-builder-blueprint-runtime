## Research Contributions

### Phase 9: Existing Workflows and Integration Points

Drafting rules should preserve:

- internal recommendation format
- workflow landing spot
- account/workflow owner control
- existing system context
- validation before action

### Phase 10: Business Models, Acquisition, and Recurring Sales

Drafts should make commercial usefulness implicit through client value. Avoid crude revenue language.

Recommendations may support renewal, expansion, proposal, assessment, compliance, lifecycle, or service work, but the note should read as client-helping and internally useful.

### Phase 11: Partner-Vendor Ecosystem

Drafts should:

- lead with client-specific reason
- keep vendor economics internal
- avoid naming vendors if validation is required
- avoid copying partner sellers by default
- treat pricing, margins, MDF, rebate, distributor, deal-registration, and partner-of-record data as sensitive

### Friday/Saturday: AI, Automation, Industry, And Signal Quality

Drafts should:

- reject or caveat weak signals
- tie AI/automation to a named workflow and owner
- name industry-specific proof when industry matters
- avoid compliance guarantees
- avoid autonomous-action claims

## Rule Guidance

### Hidden Draft Rules

Every draft should include:

- client signal
- why it matters for this client now
- relevant consultant/practice/team/asset
- why that expertise fits
- low-friction internal ask
- caveat or validation step

### Refinement Draft Rules

When refining, preserve:

- evidence strength
- relationship owner control
- no invented facts
- no forced external introduction
- no generic trend claims

### Weak Signal Behavior

If the input only says a topic exists, ask for one missing detail instead of generating a confident recommendation.

Weak examples:

- "Client mentioned AI"
- "Client is in healthcare"
- "Vendor has a promotion"
- "There are some alerts"

Strong examples:

- "Client is rolling out Copilot and has sensitive data exposure concerns"
- "Healthcare client is planning EHR migration and backup/recovery has not been discussed"
- "Vendor email-security offer matches repeated phishing tickets before QBR"
- "New acquisition creates identity and endpoint integration risk"

## Draft Rule Copy

`A useful Reasons to Connect note needs a specific signal, a client-specific implication, a relevant consultant or practice, and a small internal next step. Do not force a connection from a vague trend or topic.`

`If the signal comes from AI, automation, industry context, external news, partner/vendor data, or internal delivery work, explain why that signal changes the client situation now.`

`Keep the relationship owner in control. Do not imply client outreach, vendor outreach, deal registration, pricing use, or system updates have happened unless the user says so.`

## Tone

Compact, peer-to-peer, client-service oriented, and not salesy.
