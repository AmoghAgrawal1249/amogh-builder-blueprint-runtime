Purpose: structured setup questions asked before the model drafts the first internal recommendation.

## Export

`bringTheFirmGuide`

Target rename when implementation changes: `reasonsToConnectGuide`.

## Research Contributions

### Existing Workflows and Integration Points

Guide questions should capture where the recommendation lands: account review, QBR, proposal, renewal, delivery handoff, partner program, service issue, security review, or internal planning.

This matters because the same signal should be worded differently depending on whether it lands with an account owner, vCIO, partner lead, service owner, pursuit lead, or security lead.

### Consulting Operating Model

Guide should capture the client moment in the consulting operating model:

- discovery or diagnosis
- proposal or pursuit
- staffing or expert search
- active delivery
- steering committee or executive readout
- workshop
- renewal or follow-on work

This helps the output suggest the right kind of collaboration instead of a generic expert introduction.

### Consultant Problem-Solving and Meeting Prep

Guide should ask enough to understand the consultant's working hypothesis:

- what problem the team thinks the client has
- what evidence supports it
- what question or decision the next meeting needs to answer
- what the team is unsure about

### Consultant Delivery Formats and Diagnosis

Guide should capture whether the reason to connect is tied to a deliverable or diagnosis gap:

- current-state assessment
- options analysis
- business case
- roadmap
- operating model
- risk heatmap
- workshop pack
- proposal or SOW

### Business Models, Acquisition, and Recurring Sales

Guide should ask what kind of business motion is being supported:

- retention or renewal
- expansion or adjacent service
- assessment or discovery
- proposal or pursuit support
- compliance or risk roadmap
- partner/vendor activation
- service capacity or delivery escalation

### Partner-Vendor Ecosystem

Guide should ask whether partner/vendor data is involved and whether it needs validation before being named.

Sensitive vendor/commercial inputs include margin, rebate, MDF, pricing, deal registration, partner-of-record, renewal data, and distributor economics.

### AI, Automation, Industry, And Signal Quality

Guide should ask signal type and source:

- AI or automation
- industry/regulatory context
- external trigger
- internal delivery or service signal
- partner/vendor/tool signal
- relationship or staffing signal

Guide should also ask whether the signal is strong enough to draft from or whether more context is needed.

### Consulting Collaboration Incentives and Blockers

Guide questions should surface the political and human constraints behind collaboration:

- who owns the client relationship
- whether the first step should stay internal
- what exact help the other consultant would provide
- whether the ask is a quick review, a pressure-test, a short memo, a meeting prep input, or a client-facing introduction
- whether the recommendation could create collaboration overload, credit tension, or client-ownership sensitivity

If only a few setup questions are allowed, fold these into validation rather than adding a long questionnaire.

## Field Guidance

### Existing Program Question

Current question: does the firm already run a Bring the Firm program?

Planned direction: ask whether the firm already uses internal reasons-to-connect notes, expert-introduction programs, partner-led recommendations, account-review prompts, or similar workflows.

### Availability Question

Current question: when to confirm colleague availability.

Planned direction: broaden to validation:

- confirm availability
- confirm fit
- confirm relationship-owner approval
- confirm relationship-owner comfort with the other consultant being involved
- confirm the smallest acceptable collaboration ask
- confirm partner/vendor sensitivity
- confirm source-system data
- confirm AI-generated signal before action

### New Question Candidate: Recommendation Landing Spot

Ask where the recommendation should land:

- account owner
- vCIO/vCISO
- pursuit/proposal lead
- service delivery lead
- partner/channel owner
- procurement/licensing specialist
- platform/operator lead

### New Question Candidate: Consulting Moment

Ask where the client work is right now:

- discovery
- proposal
- delivery
- meeting prep
- executive readout
- renewal or follow-on

### New Question Candidate: Deliverable or Diagnosis Gap

Ask what the other consultant would improve:

- validate the diagnosis
- pressure-test the hypothesis
- add specialist evidence
- review scope
- sharpen the executive message
- sequence the roadmap
- identify risk before the client meeting

### New Question Candidate: Collaboration Mode

Ask what kind of help the recommendation should suggest:

- quick internal check
- pressure-test before a meeting
- short expert note
- proposal or deliverable review
- meeting prep input
- direct client introduction only if the relationship owner approves

### New Question Candidate: Signal Source

Ask what created the reason to connect:

- account or CRM note
- PSA/RMM/ticket signal
- QBR/renewal/lifecycle signal
- AI/automation signal
- industry/compliance signal
- external trigger
- partner/vendor signal

## Draft Guide Copy

Intro:

`Let's build a Reasons to Connect recommendation format. I have a few quick questions about where the note lands, what signal triggered it, and what needs validation before anyone acts.`

Core questions:

1. `Where should this recommendation land?`
2. `What client signal, hypothesis, or deliverable gap should the format handle first?`
3. `What would make this safe for the relationship owner to use or ignore?`

## Tone

Questions should be short and operational. Avoid asking about hidden model logic, scoring weights, CRM fields, or implementation details.
